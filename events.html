108 Trees Memorial – Honoring Indian Soldiers - May 26, 2018

On May 26th, 2018, the first phase of an inspiring memorial project was successfully completed in partnership with the City of London and Trees for 3 Dots. The project saw the planting of 108 trees along the Veterans Memorial Parkway in London, Ontario. This site, less than a 2-minute drive from the 401 Freeway, serves as the location for the memorial. The trees were planted in rows and columns, much like soldiers standing together in formation, symbolizing a "Living Guard of Honour" for the Indian soldiers who fought alongside the Canadian forces during World War I.

The event, which lasted approximately 2.5 hours, brought together over 120 volunteers, donors, supporters, and some Canadian Army soldiers, all working together to bring this significant project to life. The planting ceremony began at 9:00 a.m. and was inaugurated by Mr. Davinder Pal Singh, Consul (Press, Information & Community Welfare), Consulate General of India in Toronto. London City Councillor Tanya Park and the Mayor of London, Matt Brown, also joined in the event, offering their support and addressing volunteers later during the ceremony.

The memorial project not only aims to honor the sacrifices of Indian soldiers but also celebrates their deep-rooted bond with the Canadian forces. The Royal Canadian Air Force 427 Wing and the Royal Canadian Regiment were represented at the event, alongside the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), who added a meaningful touch to the ceremony.

The memorial will eventually include a stone feature, which will complete the tribute, ensuring that the sacrifices of these brave soldiers are never forgotten. This project is not just about planting trees; it’s about creating a lasting, living tribute to the bravery and unity of Indian and Canadian soldiers who fought together to protect freedom during World War I.

Media Reports on Phase 1 of the Project:

CBC News London Free Press

The 108 Trees planted represent much more than just foliage; they symbolize the shared sacrifice and the enduring legacy of the Indian and Canadian soldiers who stood side by side in the battlefields of World War I. As these trees grow, they will continue to honor their memory, serving as a testament to the power of unity, remembrance, and sacrifice.
