Brothers in Arms Memorial: A Journey Through Time

History Highlight 1

1914

The War Begins and India Joins the Fight

August 4, 1914 Britain declares war on Germany; the British Indian Army is mobilized.

September 26, 1914 The Lahore Division of the Indian Army arrives in Marseilles, France to reinforce British forces.

The Indian Army Saves the British Expeditionary Force (BEF)

1914 The Indian Army Saves the British Expeditionary Force (BEF)

September 26, 1914 The Indian Army holds the Ypres Salient, preventing a German breakthrough. Without their support, the British might have lost Belgium's channel ports, changing the course of the war.

History Highlight 2

1915

The Arrival of Canadian Troops and Joint Battles Begin

January 14, 1915 The 1st Canadian Division lands in France, joining the fight alongside Indian soldiers.

April, 1915 2nd Battle of Ypres The Canadian Expeditionary Force (CEF) faces Germany's first poison gas attack. The Lahore Division counterattacks, helping Canada withdraw and regroup.

May, 1915 Battle of Festubert Indian and Canadian troops fight side by side, securing strategic positions.

History Highlight 3

1916 - 1917

TJoint Efforts at the Somme and Vimy Ridge

1916 Battle of the Somme Indian artillery supports the 3rd & 4th Canadian Divisions, contributing to one of the most decisive battles of the war.

April, 1917 Battle of Vimy Ridge Indian cavalry and artillery play a crucial role in Canada's greatest military victory.

History Highlight 4

1918

The Final Year & Shared Sacrifice

1918 Canadian & Indian soldiers continue fighting together on the Western Front until the war's end.

November 11, 1918 The Armistice is signed, bringing World War I to a close.
