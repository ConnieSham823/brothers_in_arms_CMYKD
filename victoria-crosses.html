Bravery Beyond Borders

About Victoria Crosses

The Victoria Cross (VC) is the highest military honor awarded for extraordinary bravery in combat. Established in 1856 by Queen Victoria, the medal is given to soldiers who display valor beyond duty, often in the face of certain death.

During World War I, both Canadian and Indian soldiers fought side by side, demonstrating incredible courage on the battlefield. Their heroic actions earned them the Victoria Cross, cementing their place in history.
